# HBR-Style Case Study Format

1. **Title & Hook** — short, evocative title; one-line tension or dilemma.
2. **Protagonist & Setting** — who is facing the decision, where, when (often opens *in medias res*).
3. **Company/Industry Background** — brief context: history, market position, key players.
4. **The Situation** — the events leading up to the decision point.
5. **The Dilemma / Decision Point** — the central question the protagonist must answer (usually with a deadline or constraint).
6. **Supporting Data / Exhibits** — financials, charts, org diagrams, market data appended at the end.
7. **Stakeholder Perspectives** — quotes or viewpoints from key actors (CEO, customers, competitors, board).
8. **Options on the Table** — 2–4 possible courses of action, each with trade-offs.
9. **Resolution / Outcome** — what the protagonist actually decided, what happened next, and the measurable results (cost saved, latency reduced, tokens cut, etc.).
10. **Lessons Learned** — 3–5 takeaways the reader can apply to their own systems.
11. **Teaching Note** (separate, instructor-only) — learning objectives, suggested discussion questions, analytical frameworks.

Key conventions: written in past tense, third person, narrative (not analytical) voice, names real people and companies, and closes with the resolution and lessons so readers leave with a concrete pattern to apply.
